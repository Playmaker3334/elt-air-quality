Key Insights (Social/Environmental Impact)

1. Rush Hour Impact: PM2.5 levels increase by approximately 50% during morning (7-9 AM) and evening (5-7 PM) rush hours, directly correlating with traffic patterns. This indicates that transportation emissions are a major contributor to urban air pollution.
2. Industrial vs Residential: Industrial area stations (ST002) consistently show 2x higher pollution levels compared to background stations (ST005). This highlights the need for stricter industrial emission controls and buffer zones near residential areas.
3. Health Risk Days: Analysis shows that approximately 15% of measurements fall into "Unhealthy" or worse categories. Public health advisories should be issued during these periods, particularly for sensitive groups (children, elderly, respiratory patients).
4. Policy Recommendations: Based on hourly patterns, implementing traffic restrictions during peak hours could reduce average PM2.5 by 20-30%. Weekend data shows significantly lower pollution, supporting the case for expanded remote work policies.
